# Story E03S05: Crawler Access Policy — SEO/GEO Protection for Satellite Sites

## User Story

**As a** platform operator,
**I want** satellite sites to correctly allow SEO and GEO crawlers while blocking AI training scrapers and restricting AI answer bots from enumerating expert profiles,
**so that** Callibrate benefits from organic search traffic and AI engine recommendations without exposing its expert directory data as a free data source for AI systems that bypass the prospect funnel.

---

## Context

Two crawler categories must be handled differently — and they are not the same thing:

**Category 1 — SEO bots** (Googlebot, Bingbot): full access — traditional search indexing.

**Category 2 — AI training bots** (GPTBot, CCBot, Google-Extended, anthropic-ai, ClaudeBot): blocked entirely — expert profiles must not be baked into future LLM weights, from which queries could be answered without attribution or traffic.

**Category 3 — AI answer/search bots** (PerplexityBot, OAI-SearchBot): partial access — platform and category pages allowed (GEO: AI systems can recommend "go to Callibrate to find an expert"), but individual expert profile URLs blocked. This prevents an AI engine from directly listing experts from the directory in response to a query like "find me an n8n expert", bypassing the prospect funnel.

The Cloudflare zone dashboard setting ("Block AI training bots → Block on all pages") applies globally at the CDN layer and must be activated manually by the founder for the satellite domain zone. This is a belt-and-suspenders complement to robots.txt — not a replacement.

Profile pages remain fully crawlable by SEO bots (traditional search indexing is desirable). The restriction applies only to AI-category bots.

---

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] AC1: Each satellite site root exposes a statically generated `robots.txt` at `/robots.txt` containing, at minimum:
  ```
  User-agent: *
  Allow: /

  # AI training bots — blocked entirely
  User-agent: GPTBot
  Disallow: /

  User-agent: CCBot
  Disallow: /

  User-agent: Google-Extended
  Disallow: /

  User-agent: anthropic-ai
  Disallow: /

  User-agent: ClaudeBot
  Disallow: /

  # AI answer/search bots — platform pages allowed, expert profiles blocked
  User-agent: PerplexityBot
  Allow: /
  Disallow: /experts/
  Disallow: /profiles/

  User-agent: OAI-SearchBot
  Allow: /
  Disallow: /experts/
  Disallow: /profiles/

  Sitemap: https://[satellite_domain]/sitemap.xml
  ```
  The exact paths (`/experts/`, `/profiles/`) must match the actual URL structure of the satellite site once its routing is defined.

- [ ] AC2: The `robots.txt` is generated by the satellite Worker (E06S14) per request and served with `Cache-Control: public, max-age=86400` — CF-edge-cached across all POPs, functionally equivalent to static. *(Supersedes original Astro build-time assumption — DEC-54)*

- [ ] AC3: A `sitemap.xml` is generated at build time and linked in `robots.txt`. The sitemap includes: homepage, category/vertical pages, static content pages. Individual expert profile URLs (`/experts/:id`, `/profiles/:slug` or equivalent) are **excluded** from the sitemap (crawlable by SEO bots but not actively signalled via sitemap).

- [ ] AC4: Expert profile pages do NOT carry `<meta name="robots" content="noindex">` — they must remain indexable by Googlebot/Bingbot. The restriction is robots.txt-only for AI bots.

- [ ] AC5: `SETUP.md` documents the mandatory manual founder action: *"In Cloudflare dashboard → [satellite domain zone] → Security → Bots → AI Training Bots: select 'Block on all pages'."*

---

## Out of Scope

- `llms.txt` (emerging standard, not yet stable — post-MVP)
- Structured data / schema.org markup for expert profiles (separate Story)
- Open Graph / Twitter Card meta tags (separate Story)
- callibrate.io and app.callibrate.io (expert-facing domains — separate policy)
- Robots policy for the Cloudflare Workers API layer (`api.callibrate.io`) — API routes are not crawlable by definition

## Notes

- The Cloudflare toggle acts at the CDN/IP level and blocks bots regardless of robots.txt compliance. `robots.txt` remains necessary for compliant bots that respect the standard. Both layers are needed.
- Bots that ignore robots.txt (some low-quality scrapers, non-compliant crawlers) are handled by Cloudflare's bot management layer — not by this Story.
- The list of AI training bots is evolving. At MVP, the above list covers the major actors. A maintenance task should be scheduled post-launch to review new entrants (e.g. Apple's Applebot-Extended if repurposed for training).
- The distinction between "training" and "answer" bots is not always clear-cut. When in doubt, err toward restriction on profile paths.
