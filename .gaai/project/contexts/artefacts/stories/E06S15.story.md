# Story E06S15: Email deliverability hardening — subdomain, DMARC, reply-to, plain text

## User Story

**As a** platform operator,
**I want** transactional emails sent from a dedicated subdomain with proper authentication, a monitored reply-to address, and a plain text fallback,
**so that** emails reach the inbox reliably, the root domain reputation is isolated from ESP activity, and the infrastructure supports future marketing email on a separate subdomain without cross-contamination.

---

## Context

E06S06 delivered the email-notifications queue consumer using Resend. The current implementation sends from `notifications@callibrate.io` (root domain), without `reply_to`, without plain text version, and without DMARC.

Analysis of Resend deliverability best practices against the codebase identified 5 improvements:
- **DMARC** — Google (Nov 2025) and Microsoft (May 2025) reject bulk senders without DMARC. Even at low volume, DMARC is trivial to set up and provides authentication visibility.
- **Sending subdomain** — isolates ESP reputation from root domain. Critical because marketing email is planned for the future — transactional and marketing must be on separate subdomains from day one.
- **Reply-to** — UX improvement: experts and prospects who reply to confirmation emails should reach a monitored address instead of the void.
- **Plain text** — accessibility improvement for screen readers, Apple Watch, and text-mode email clients.

SPF for Resend is already configured.

## Notes

**DMARC progression for single-ESP transactional sender:**
- `p=none` = monitoring only (satisfies Google/Microsoft minimum requirement)
- `p=reject` = full protection (emails failing authentication are rejected)
- With a single ESP (Resend) and no other email sources, the progression from `p=none` to `p=reject` can happen within days — there are no other legitimate senders to accidentally block.

**Why `env.EMAIL_FROM_DOMAIN` instead of hardcoding:**
- Staging may use a different sending domain or the root domain before the subdomain is verified
- Production uses `send.callibrate.io`
- Avoids code changes when switching domains

**Future marketing email architecture (not in this story):**
- Transactional: `send.callibrate.io` (Resend)
- Marketing: `news.callibrate.io` or `mail.callibrate.io` (separate ESP or separate Resend domain)
- Reputation isolation between the two streams is the primary reason for subdomain separation
