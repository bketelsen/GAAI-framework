# Story E01S06: Expert-Facing Acquisition Landing Page

## User Story

**As an** AI integration expert discovering Callibrate,
**I want** to understand immediately what Callibrate offers me, how I get paid, and what it costs,
**so that** I can decide confidently whether to sign up — with zero ambiguity about the business model.

---

## Context

Callibrate's expert-facing landing page is the primary conversion surface for expert acquisition. It is the page an expert lands on after clicking a hand raiser reply, a community mention, or a direct invite. It must:

1. Communicate the value proposition in the first 5 seconds
2. Explain the pricing model with **crystal clear** transparency (DEC-131 §6) — experts must understand *before* signing up that they pay per booking for Callibrate-originated prospects and pay nothing for their own direct traffic
3. Convert interested experts to `/signup`

This page lives on `callibrate.io` (same landing Worker as E02S09, `workers/frontend/landing/`). Route: `GET /experts` or `GET /pour-les-experts` (FR-first, slug TBD).

**Dependency note:** Full AC definition is deferred until E01 validation gates (E01S04) are passed and the promise language is locked. This placeholder captures the mandatory structure and non-negotiable requirements only.

## Acceptance Criteria

### Structure & Content (placeholder — to be refined post-E01S04)

- [ ] AC1: Hero section (above the fold):
  - Headline targeting expert pain point (locked via E01S01 promise language)
  - Sub-headline stating the core offer in one sentence
  - Primary CTA: "Rejoindre la liste d'attente" (pre-launch) / "Créer mon profil" (post-launch) → `/signup`
  - No pricing table above the fold — lead with value, not cost

- [ ] AC2: "Comment ça marche" section — 3-step visual flow:
  1. Vous créez votre profil (compétences, tarif, disponibilités)
  2. Callibrate vous envoie des prospects qualifiés (matching engine)
  3. Vous décrochez des missions sans prospecter

- [ ] AC3: **Pricing section — non-negotiable transparency (DEC-131 §6).** Must be a named, findable section (anchor `#tarifs` or `#facturation`). Required content — no paraphrasing that creates ambiguity:
  - **Callibrate vous trouve un prospect → vous payez par booking réussi.** (tarif : [montant DEC-67, à confirmer]) — pas d'abonnement, pas de frais fixes.
  - **Vous partagez votre lien direct → le prospect vient à vous → gratuit.** Votre lien Callibrate sur votre site, LinkedIn, Google Business Profile = aucun frais, quelle que soit la fréquence.
  - Visual clarity: two columns or two clearly labelled blocks — "Prospect Callibrate" (payant) vs "Votre lien direct" (gratuit).
  - No dark patterns: both cases must be equally visible, same font weight. The free case must not be hidden in fine print.

- [ ] AC4: Social proof section (placeholder at launch — populate once data exists):
  - Expert testimonials (3 minimum — collect post-M1)
  - Stats: "X experts actifs" / "Y missions décrocheés" — show only when real data available; placeholder at launch: hide section or show "Rejoignez les premiers experts en beta"

- [ ] AC5: FAQ section (minimum 4 questions):
  - "Combien est-ce que je paie ?" → réponse = pricing rule (AC3)
  - "Qu'est-ce que le lien direct ?" → réponse = description du lien + confirmation que c'est gratuit
  - "Callibrate correspond-il à mon profil ?" → réponse = types d'experts acceptés
  - "Puis-je refuser des prospects ?" → réponse = oui, aucun engagement

- [ ] AC6: Footer CTA: "Créer mon profil gratuit" → `/signup`.

### Technical
