## I. CREDIBILITY SIGNALING ARCHITECTURE

### AKU-CRED001 — Quantified Epistemic Shift

**Type:** Structural Industry Transition
**Evidence:** Theoretical + Platform Convergence
**Confidence:** High

Credibility has shifted from qualitative reputation to machine-verifiable technical signals.

Modern trust is constructed through:

* Structured metadata
* Named attribution
* Citation density
* Freshness signals
* Technical integrity

**Strategic Implication:**
Trust must be operationalized through measurable, extractable signals.

**GAAI mapping:** Foundational principle for the entire content blueprint. Every skill in the pipeline contributes credibility signals: CNT-001 (source quality), CNT-003 (citation integration), CNT-005 (structured data), CNT-006 (extraction-ready formatting), CNT-009 (credibility scoring).

---

### AKU-CRED002 — Provenance Transparency (Who / How / Why)

**Type:** Normative Standard
**Confidence:** High

Trust requires explicit clarity on:

* **Who** authored the content
* **How** it was created (including AI disclosure)
* **Why** it exists (help users vs manipulate systems)

**Optimization Standard:**

* Visible author pages
* AI usage disclosure where applicable
* Clear editorial mission alignment

**Cross-ref:** Deepens HMN-001 AKU-AUTH013 (Transparency Standard) with the specific Who/How/Why framework.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-005-seo-optimize must include author schema markup. `memory/domains/content-production/voice-guide.md` should define the editorial mission statement for the "Why" dimension.

---

### AKU-CRED003 — Interface-Driven Persuasion Effect

**Type:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** High

Persistent, aligned sidebars adjacent to core content significantly increase agreement change in readers compared to hidden or footer citations.

**Mechanism:**
Source visibility enhances cognitive anchoring.

**Implementation Rule:**
High-trust content should surface source context in-line or in persistent UI elements.

**GAAI mapping:** Dual scope — content formatting + UI design. For content skills: CNT-003-draft should integrate citations inline (parenthetical or adjacent) rather than as endnotes. For UI: `coding/` blueprint concern for blog template design.

---

### AKU-CRED004 — Technical Baseline Integrity

**Scope: `coding/` blueprint — context only for content**

**Type:** Normative Infrastructure Requirement
**Confidence:** High

HTTPS and secure infrastructure are no longer trust enhancers — they are baseline requirements.

Absence signals credibility deficiency.

**GAAI mapping:** Not actionable in content blueprint. Infrastructure requirement for `coding/` blueprint. Content skills should be aware that publishing on non-HTTPS undermines all credibility work.

---

### AKU-CRED005 — Named Source Premium

**Type:** Normative Journalistic Standard
**Confidence:** High

Named sources carry accountability and credibility weight.

Anonymous sources:

* Classified as weakest form
* Used only when necessary
* Explicitly labeled as anonymous

**Operational Rule:**
Prioritize named institutional or expert attribution.

**Cross-ref:** Extends SEO-001 AKU-002 (Trust Dominance — cite primary sources) and AGT-001 WF-AI02 (Multi-Tier Source Validation) with the journalistic named-source standard.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-001-research must prioritize named sources. Research brief artefact should tag each source as `[named: Person/Org]` or `[unnamed]`. CNT-009-quality-gate should flag content with >50% unnamed sources.

## II. CITATION DENSITY & STRUCTURAL PLACEMENT

### AKU-CRED006 — Statistics & Institutional Citation Lift

**Type:** Causal Evidence
**Confidence:** High

Content incorporating:

* Quantitative statistics
* Expert quotations
* Recognized institutional citations (.gov, .edu)

Shows significant increases in AI visibility.

**Optimization Rule:**
Embed measurable, verifiable claims.

**Cross-ref:** Complements GEO-001 AKU-GEO006 (Specificity Premium — Concreteness Bias mechanism) and SEO-001 AKU-011 (Quantitative Fact Density). Adds the specific institutional citation (.gov/.edu) angle.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-001-research must actively seek institutional sources (Tier 1 per AGT-001 WF-AI02). CNT-003-draft must embed statistics with full attribution.

---

### AKU-CRED007 — AI-Era Citation Density Threshold

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

AI-optimized content requires higher citation density than traditional SEO.

Indicative pattern:

* ~8–12 external citations per 1,500 words
* Approximately one citation every 150–200 words

**Guardrail:**
Density must serve clarity, not artificial inflation.

**GAAI mapping:** Concrete metric for CNT-006-geo-optimize and CNT-009-quality-gate. Quality gate should count citations per word and flag content below threshold. CNT-001-research must produce research briefs with sufficient source volume (minimum 10-15 sources for a 1500-word piece).

---

### AKU-CRED008 — Front-Loading Citation Bias ("Ski Ramp" Pattern)

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

AI citation probability is front-loaded. Peak citation likelihood occurs within the first third of content.

**Optimization Rule:**
Place in the first 30% of content:

* Statistics
* Named sources
* High-entity explanations

**Cross-ref:** Complements GEO-001 AKU-GEO005 (BLUF — answer-first) with the citation-specific angle. BLUF is about answer placement; Ski Ramp is about evidence placement. Both point to front-loading.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-002-outline must structure the first third as evidence-dense. CNT-003-draft follows the outline's front-loaded authority architecture. CNT-006-geo-optimize validates the ski ramp pattern is respected.

---

### AKU-CRED009 — Answer Capsule Isolation

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** Moderate

Concise, self-contained 40–60 word explanations immediately following headings show high citation likelihood.

Notably:
Many cited capsules contain no outbound links.

**Hypothesis:**
Links may signal answer incompleteness to AI extraction systems.

**Guardrail:**
Avoid stripping links unnaturally; apply capsule design strategically.

**Cross-ref:** Extends GEO-001 AKU-GEO015 (Nugget Preference — 40-60 word sections) and AGT-001 AKU-AI010 (Citation Capsules). Adds the new insight: outbound links within capsules may reduce citation probability.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-006-geo-optimize formatting rule — create self-contained answer capsules after key H2s. Keep outbound links in elaboration sections, not in the capsule itself.

## III. E-E-A-T & AUTHORITY SYSTEMS

**Scope: strategy / off-page — context only for content**

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** Moderate

Domain Authority (DA 70+) functions as a primary retrieval gate for inclusion in AI source pools.

**Implication:**
High-authority domains enter retrieval sets more consistently.

**Guardrail:**
DA is gating, not a citation guarantee.

**Cross-ref:** Related to SEO-001 AKU-014 (Backlink Weight) and GEO-001 AKU-GEO002 (Earned Media Preference).

**Scope: strategy / off-page — context only for content**

**Cross-ref:** Related to SEO-001 AKU-015 (Brand Mentions & Authority Graph) and GEO-001 AKU-GEO013 (Brand Authority Correlation).

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

Content updated within recent windows significantly outperforms older content.

**Operational Rule:**
Implement systematic refresh cycles.

**Cross-ref:** Extends WRT-001 AKU-W015 (Freshness Multiplier) and GEO-001 AKU-GEO009 (Machine-Readable Freshness Signals). Adds the "systematic refresh cycles" operational rule.

### AKU-CRED015 — Automated Verification Classification

**Type:** Empirical Measurement
**Confidence:** High

Modern verification systems classify claims into:

* Supported
* Partially Supported
* Unsupported
* Uncertain

**Operational Insight:**
Structured claim auditing reduces hallucination propagation.

**GAAI mapping:** Directly actionable for CNT-009-quality-gate. During fact-verification pass, each major claim in the draft should be classified using this taxonomy. Claims classified as "Unsupported" or "Uncertain" must be either removed, sourced, or explicitly qualified.

**Trust Mechanism:**

## SAFE CREDIBILITY OPTIMIZATION PROTOCOL

When generating credibility strategies through the content blueprint:

1. Separate normative standards from correlational findings.
2. Avoid over-optimizing for citation density without value. (CRED007 guardrail)
3. Balance entity richness with readability.
4. Maintain named-source discipline. (CRED005)
5. Ensure all statistical claims are verifiable. (CRED006)
6. Avoid artificial front-loading if it harms coherence. (CRED008 guardrail)
