## I. DETECTABLE AI LINGUISTIC SIGNALS

### AKU-AUTH001 — Low Perplexity Signature

**Type:** Technical Detection Signal
**Evidence:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** High
**Causality:** Established

AI-generated text exhibits significantly lower median perplexity than human writing, making it statistically more predictable.

**Mechanism:**
Large language models optimize for probability maximization → reduced entropy → predictable phrasing.

**Detection Risk:**
Low perplexity increases statistical detectability via metrics such as MAUVE or cross-perplexity analysis.

**Guardrail for Generation:**
Introduce controlled lexical and syntactic variability without artificial randomness.

**GAAI mapping:** Execution rule for CNT-003-draft (vary phrasing intentionally) and validation criterion for CNT-004-edit (flag overly predictable passages).

---

### AKU-AUTH002 — Reduced Structural Burstiness

**Type:** Observational Detection Signal
**Confidence:** High

AI text often shows:

* Uniform sentence length
* Consistent clause structure
* Rhythmic regularity

Humans exhibit higher structural variance ("burstiness").

**Mitigation Strategy:**
Introduce:

* Variable sentence lengths
* Occasional fragment structures
* Asymmetrical rhythm

Avoid artificial chaos.

**GAAI mapping:** Editorial checklist item for CNT-004-edit — verify sentence length variance across paragraphs. Flag sections where >5 consecutive sentences have similar length.

---

### AKU-AUTH003 — Nominal Loading Bias

**Type:** Correlational Detection Marker
**Confidence:** High

AI writing displays elevated usage of:

* Nouns
* Noun phrases
* Coordinating conjunctions
* "Difficult" vocabulary

Humans use:

* More pronouns
* More auxiliary verbs
* More interpersonal markers

**Risk:**
Excess nominal density creates "institutional stiffness."

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-004-edit should check pronoun-to-noun ratio and flag passages with excessive nominal density. CNT-003-draft prompt should instruct: "use pronouns naturally, avoid institutional tone."

---

### AKU-AUTH004 — Canonical SVO Rigidity

**Type:** Observational
**Confidence:** Moderate

AI outputs show strong adherence to Subject-Verb-Object ordering.

Humans introduce:

* Inversions
* Subordinate clause openings
* Fragmented rhetorical constructions

**Mitigation:**
Controlled syntactic diversity improves authenticity perception.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-004-edit editorial rule — introduce 2-3 syntactic inversions per 500 words where they serve rhetorical purpose (not randomly).

## II. HUMAN AUTHENTICITY SIGNALS

### AKU-AUTH005 — Interactive Register Marker

**Type:** Correlational Authenticity Signal
**Confidence:** High

Authentic writing correlates with:

* Second-person address
* Pronoun density
* Verb tense variation
* Dialogic phrasing

**Perception Impact:**
Higher perceived "humanness" and engagement.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-003-draft prompt instruction — use second-person address where appropriate to content type. CNT-004-edit validates dialogic register presence.

**Cross-ref:** Complements WRT-001 AKU-W004 (Audience Self-Interest — reader-centric framing) and AKU-W018 (Hero Reversal).

---

### AKU-AUTH006 — Logical Thread Integrity

**Type:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** High

Expert evaluators detect human writing through:

* Coherent argumentative thread
* Low redundancy
* Non-repetitive phrasing

AI writing frequently suffers from:

* Stylistic repetition
* Paraphrased redundancy
* Circular explanation

**Optimization Rule:**
Enforce argument progression validation during editing.

**GAAI mapping:** Critical validation for CNT-004-edit — scan for paraphrased redundancy (same idea restated differently) and circular explanations. CNT-009-quality-gate should include "argument progression" as scored dimension.

**Cross-ref:** Complements AGT-001 AKU-AI003 (Context Stitching) — section-by-section drafting reduces but doesn't eliminate this risk. Editorial pass is the safety net.

---

### AKU-AUTH007 — Lexical Diversity Indicator

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

High Type-Token Ratios (TTR) correlate with perceived expertise.

AI models often use complex words but demonstrate lower overall lexical diversity.

**Guardrail:**
Avoid forced synonym injection. Prioritize semantic variation aligned with context.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-004-edit can use TTR as a heuristic quality check. Flag passages with low diversity. CNT-003-draft prompt should avoid thesaurus-style synonym rotation.

## III. ENGAGEMENT & PERCEPTION DYNAMICS

### AKU-AUTH008 — AI Authorship Penalty

**Type:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** High

Disclosure of AI authorship decreases engagement and preference scores — even when content quality is identical.

**Psychological Mechanism:**
Attribution bias favoring human identity.

**Implication:**
Perception is not purely quality-based; identity framing matters.

**Cross-ref:** Provides causal experimental evidence for WRT-001 AKU-W014 (Trust Premium — Human vs AI Perception). W014 observes the correlation; AUTH008 establishes the causal mechanism.

**GAAI mapping:** Strategic context for content publication decisions. Reinforces the imperative of CNT-004-edit humanization pass — content must not read as AI-generated regardless of disclosure policy.

---

### AKU-AUTH009 — Readability Paradox

**Type:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** Moderate

AI text often demonstrates:

* Higher syntactic complexity
* Lower readability scores
* Greater cognitive load

**Result:**
Long-form AI text feels "convoluted" despite grammatical correctness.

**Mitigation:**
Prioritize clarity over syntactic density.

**Cross-ref:** Complements WRT-001 AKU-W017 (Visual Readability Optimization) — W017 covers formatting, AUTH009 covers AI-specific syntactic complexity.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-004-edit must check readability score (Flesch-Kincaid or equivalent) and simplify passages exceeding target complexity for the audience.

---

### AKU-AUTH010 — Hybrid Parity Effect

**Type:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** High

AI-human collaboration performs equivalently to human-only content in:

* Engagement
* Brand attitude
* Purchase intent

**Key Insight:**
Hybrid refinement mitigates the AI-authorship penalty.

**Cross-ref:** Validates AGT-001 AKU-AI007 (AI Slop Threshold — human review raises score above 90). AUTH010 provides the experimental proof that the hybrid model works.

**GAAI mapping:** Validates the content blueprint architecture: AI draft (CNT-003) → human editorial pass (CNT-004) → quality gate (CNT-009). The pipeline is not a compromise — it's the optimal model per experimental evidence.

---

### AKU-AUTH011 — Skimming Effect

**Type:** Causal Experimental
**Confidence:** Moderate

Readers process AI text faster but with lower cognitive engagement.

AI text is:

* Easier to skim
* Less persuasive
* Less emotionally immersive

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-004-edit must introduce friction points that slow reading and increase engagement: rhetorical questions, unexpected data, personal anecdotes, perspective shifts. Pure AI drafts are "too smooth."

## VII. SAFE GENERATION PROTOCOL

When generating content through the content blueprint:

1. Introduce syntactic variance intentionally. (AUTH001, AUTH002, AUTH004)
2. Reduce noun-density in informational prose. (AUTH003)
3. Maintain argumentative progression tracking. (AUTH006)
4. Avoid excessive clause chaining. (AUTH009)
5. Include subjective or contextual nuance where appropriate. (AUTH005, AUTH011)
6. Always validate claims before publication. (AUTH018, AUTH014)

**GAAI mapping:** Rules 1-5 are CNT-003-draft execution constraints. Rule 6 is CNT-009-quality-gate mandatory check.
