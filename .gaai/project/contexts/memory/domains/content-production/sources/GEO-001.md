## I. GENERATIVE ENGINE SELECTION LOGIC

### AKU-GEO001 — Engine-Specific Quality Threshold Variance

**Type:** Experimental Finding
**Confidence:** High
**Causality:** Observed Behavioral Difference

Generative engines (GEs) do not apply uniform citation standards.

Observed patterns:

* Brave Summary and Google AI Overviews exhibit higher citation thresholds.
* Perplexity demonstrates lower average quality threshold for citation.

**Operational Implication:**
Optimization must be engine-aware. A strategy effective for one engine may not generalize across all.

**Guardrail:**
Do not assume universal citation probability across engines.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-006-geo-optimize should include engine-specific checklists, not a one-size-fits-all approach.

---

### AKU-GEO002 — Earned Media Preference Bias

**Type:** Correlational Platform Observation
**Confidence:** High
**Causality:** Correlated

Generative engines preferentially cite:

* Government domains (.gov)
* Educational institutions (.edu)
* Established media outlets
* Recognized third-party authorities

Brand-owned blogs and social content are cited less frequently.

**Strategic Implication:**
Citation likelihood increases when:

* Your content references primary authoritative sources.
* Your brand earns mentions on high-authority domains.

**GAAI mapping:** Informs both CNT-001-research (source tier selection) and content strategy (pursue earned media placements).

---

### AKU-GEO003 — Information Gain Requirement

**Type:** Experimental
**Confidence:** High
**Causality:** Probable

Models prioritize sources that contain:

* Unique data points
* Proprietary insights
* Non-commoditized information

**Optimization Rule:**
Content must introduce incremental informational value beyond common knowledge.

**Failure Mode:**
Repackaged, generic summaries of already-saturated topics.

**GAAI mapping:** Reinforces AGT-001 AKU-AI015R (proprietary knowledge injection) and AKU-AI019 (RAG differentiation). Memory assets in `content/` become a GEO competitive advantage.

---

### AKU-GEO004 — Intent Alignment Bias

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

AI Overviews disproportionately trigger for informational queries. Longer queries show higher AIO activation rates.

**Operational Implication:**
GEO is strongest in informational, research-driven search contexts. Transactional and navigational queries remain SEO-dominant.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-001-research must classify target query intent. GEO optimization effort should be proportional to informational intent density.

## II. STRUCTURAL EXTRACTION SIGNALS

### AKU-GEO005 — Answer-First (BLUF) Architecture

**Type:** Experimental
**Confidence:** High
**Impact:** Increased citation probability

Placing a direct answer at the beginning of a section increases extractability.

**Implementation Standard:**

* First 1–2 sentences = direct, self-contained answer.
* Follow with supporting elaboration.

**Guardrail:**
Avoid artificial BLUF placement that compromises readability.

**Cross-ref:** Deepens SEO-001 AKU-009 with experimental evidence and specific sentence-count standard.

**GAAI mapping:** Mandatory formatting rule in CNT-006-geo-optimize checklist.

---

### AKU-GEO006 — The Specificity Premium

**Type:** Experimental
**Confidence:** High
**Causality:** Probable

Adding quantitative data significantly increases citation likelihood.

Mechanism hypothesis:
Numerical values attract higher model attention weights (Concreteness Bias).

**Model Rule:**
Prioritize verifiable statistics over qualitative descriptors.

**Critical Safeguard:**
Never fabricate or inflate statistics.

**Cross-ref:** Complements SEO-001 AKU-011 (Quantitative Fact Density) and WRT-001 AKU-W003 (Specificity Bias). Adds the "Concreteness Bias" mechanism hypothesis.

---

### AKU-GEO007 — Semantic Hierarchical Integration

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

Proper heading hierarchy (H1 > H2 > H3) increases citation probability.

Generative engines use semantic boundaries as chunking markers.

**Operational Standard:**
Maintain strict hierarchical nesting. Never skip heading levels.

**Cross-ref:** Deepens SEO-001 AKU-008 with the "chunking markers" mechanism specific to generative engines.

---

### AKU-GEO008 — Schema-Driven Extraction

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** Moderate

FAQPage schema increases structured extraction likelihood.

**Implication:**
Schema facilitates parsing but does not guarantee inclusion.

**Cross-ref:** Aligns with AGT-001 AKU-AI012 and SEO-001 UZ-001 (Schema Markup Causality). GEO angle: schema aids machine extraction, distinct from ranking impact.

---

### AKU-GEO009 — Machine-Readable Freshness Signals

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

Visible timestamps and JSON-LD dateModified strongly correlate with citation.

**Optimization Requirement:**
Expose freshness signals in both human-visible format and structured metadata.

**GAAI mapping:** Technical checklist item for CNT-005-seo-optimize (implementation) verified by CNT-006-geo-optimize.

## III. TRAFFIC & ECONOMIC IMPACT PATTERNS

### AKU-GEO010 — CTR Erosion Effect

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

Presence of AI Overviews reduces organic CTR for top-ranking pages.

**Implication:**
Traffic volume declines, but not necessarily traffic value.

**Cross-ref:** Extends SEO-001 AKU-006. Read with AKU-GEO011 below for complete economic model.

---

### AKU-GEO011 — High-Intent Conversion Amplification

**Type:** Experimental (Case Studies)
**Confidence:** High

AI-referred traffic exhibits significantly higher conversion rates.

**Strategic Shift:**
Optimize for qualified exposure, not raw click volume.

**GAAI mapping:** GEO010 + GEO011 together redefine success metrics. Content quality gate (CNT-009) should weight conversion potential over traffic volume.

---

### AKU-GEO012 — Citation Independence from Rankings

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** High

Most AI citations originate from domains outside the top organic positions.

**Critical Insight:**
GEO visibility is partially decoupled from traditional rank authority.

**GAAI mapping:** Validates GEO as a separate optimization pass (CNT-006) rather than a subset of SEO (CNT-005). A page can rank poorly in traditional SERP but be cited by AI engines.

---

### AKU-GEO013 — Brand Authority Correlation

**Type:** Correlational
**Confidence:** Moderate

Brand search volume correlates with AI citation frequency.

**Implication:**
Off-page brand strength remains a gating factor. Content optimization alone is insufficient without brand authority signals.

## IV. SEO VS GEO STRATEGIC DIVERGENCES

### AKU-GEO014 — Keyword Density Devaluation

**Type:** Experimental
**Confidence:** High

Keyword stuffing decreases visibility in generative engines. GEs prioritize entity alignment over lexical repetition.

**Cross-ref:** Extends SEO-001 AKU-012 (Topical Coverage vs Keyword Density) into GEO territory.

**GAAI mapping:** CNT-006-geo-optimize must actively flag and remove keyword stuffing that CNT-005-seo-optimize might tolerate.

---

### AKU-GEO015 — Nugget Preference over Mega-Guides

**Type:** Observational
**Confidence:** Moderate

Generative engines favor self-contained, fact-dense sections over sprawling mega-guides.

**Optimization Pattern:**
Design extractable "knowledge nuggets" — self-contained paragraphs that answer a specific question completely within 40-60 words.

**GAAI mapping:** Formatting rule for CNT-006-geo-optimize. Aligns with SEO-001 AKU-008 (Semantic Chunking).

---

### AKU-GEO016 — Interaction Model Shift

**Type:** Conceptual Framework
**Confidence:** High

SEO Model: Search → Click → Read
GEO Model: Prompt → Synthesis → Verification

Goal shifts from "earning the click" to "earning the citation."

**GAAI mapping:** Strategic context for blueprint design. The content pipeline must produce assets optimized for both models simultaneously.

---

### AKU-GEO017 — Source Proximity Strategy

**Type:** Experimental
**Confidence:** High

Explicitly citing recognized authorities increases citation reciprocity probability.

**Mechanism:**
Models identify authority adjacency — content that cites authoritative sources inherits trust signal proximity.

**GAAI mapping:** Rule for CNT-003-draft and CNT-006-geo-optimize: every major claim must cite a Tier 1 or Tier 2 source (per AGT-001 WF-AI02 source tiers).

**Type:** Composite Model
**Confidence:** Moderate

Cross-engine citation consistency appears associated with:

* GEO Quality Score ≥ 0.70
* Strong metadata
* Semantic HTML compliance
* Structured data completeness

Threshold models are probabilistic, not deterministic guarantees.

## VII. MODEL-SAFE OPTIMIZATION RULES

When generating GEO recommendations:

1. Classify recommendation by evidence type:
   * Experimental
   * Correlational
   * Speculative

2. Avoid numeric absolutism.

3. Separate optimization dimensions:
   * Extraction optimization (structure, formatting)
   * Authority optimization (sourcing, citations)
   * Intent alignment (query type matching)
   * Brand strength effects (off-page signals)

4. Never recommend:
   * Keyword stuffing
   * Artificial statistic injection
   * Schema spam
